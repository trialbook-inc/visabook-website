Temporary Residence

Temporary Residence to Canada allows people to come to Canada for a limited period without becoming permanent residents. Its purpose is to let individuals visit, study, or work in Canada while contributing to tourism, education, and the workforce—without a long-term immigration commitment.

Temporary Residence makes roughly 7% of the Canadian population!

Explore, Study, and Work: Your Canadian Journey Starts Here

Study Permit & Extensions

A Canadian Study Permit allows international students to attend designated institutions, offering world-class education, cultural exchange, and professional growth while building a strategic foundation for work permits and permanent residency.

Visitor Visa (TRV)

A Visitor TRV allows nationals from visa-required countries to enter Canada for tourism, family, or business. It offers first-hand experience and networking while providing flexible entry for up to six months.

Visitor Visa (ETA)

An eTA allows visa-exempt travelers quick, affordable entry to Canada for tourism, study or for business. Valid for five years, it facilitates multiple short visits and provides a convenient pathway for international travelers.

Super Visa

The Super Visa allows parents and grandparents of Canadians extended five-year stays. This multiple-entry visa fosters family reunification while ensuring peace of mind through mandatory health insurance and long-term residency flexibility.

Post-Graduate Work Permit​

The PGWP enables international graduates from designated institutions to gain professional Canadian work experience. This open permit supports career growth and serves as a vital stepping stone toward achieving permanent residency.

International Experience Canada (IEC)

The IEC program allows young adults from partner countries to work and travel across Canada. Participants gain global experience and professional growth through working holidays, internships, or young professional career streams.

Business Visitor

A Business Visitor allows foreign nationals to enter Canada temporarily to conduct business activities, such as meetings or conferences, without entering the Canadian labour market or requiring a work permit.

Temporary status extensions & restorations​

Canadian immigration options that let eligible applicants extend temporary status or restore expired status, maintaining legal stay in Canada while addressing processing delays, missed deadlines, or changing personal circumstances.

Short Term Work Permit

Canada’s short-term work visa lets foreign workers enter temporarily to perform specific jobs, often employer-specific, for limited periods, sometimes exempt from a work permit under international agreements or special programs.
