Justice for Your Journey

Temporary Residence Appeals

While temporary visas have no formal appeal division, applicants can challenge refusals through Judicial Review at the Federal Court or a Request for Reconsideration. These pathways allow you to address officer errors and ensure your application receives fair, legal treatment.

Permanent Residence Appeals

Permanent Residence Appeals offer a critical legal lifeline for individuals facing sponsorship refusals or loss of status. Through the Immigration Appeal Division (IAD), applicants can present new evidence and humanitarian arguments to overturn unfair decisions and secure their future in Canada.

Citizenship Appeals

Citizenship Appeals allow applicants to challenge a refusal or revocation in the Federal Court. This process ensures that decisions regarding residency obligations, language requirements, or "good character" are made fairly, providing a vital legal remedy to protect your path to becoming Canadian.
